About The Project

The Good Governance Project was founded through a partnership with BCâ€™s Clough Center for Constitutional Democracy. The Project consists of issue-specific Working Groups tackling non-partisan democracy reforms (ranked-choice voting, campaign finance, gerrymandering, etc.). We want to empower and channel student energy on critical democracy reform issues.

In the Fall, Working Groups explore issues and weigh solutions. They spend the remainder of the school year aggregating the student voice to advance specific legislative action.

The GGP Leadership Team is tasked with creating a framework for Working Groups, so that they can effectively learn about common ground issues in our political system, identify a specific solution and advocate for it. The Leadership Team provides networks, technology, and structures to help gather support from diverse, interdisciplinary student and constituent groups, faculty and state politicians.

We believe that the rules that govern our political systems can and should be improved so that they provide better results for the public interest.
